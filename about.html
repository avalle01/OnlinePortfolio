How I Got Here

So, it all started with me taking the leap and heading abroad for college - where else can a young individual thrive other than the place where the "Bright lights will inspire you"? I was hungry for adventure and knowledge. Enticed by its potential, I pursued Computer Science because it felt like the ultimate playground for creativity and innovation. The idea of being part of groundbreaking research just lit a fire in me. I found myself constantly drawn to the realm of data, fascinated by its power to unveil insights and drive decision-making.

Along the way, I saw firsthand the struggles women face in the tech field, and it sparked my drive to push for more diversity and inclusivity. These experiences have shaped my journey and fueled my determination to contribute a positive impact in the tech scene. Learn more about my academic journey!

Society of Women Engineers

During my undergraduate years at New York Tech, I had the privilege of serving as both a member and Vice President of SWE. This allowed me to actively contribute to a dynamic professional organization committed to empowering women in engineering and technology. Through SWE, I accessed invaluable resources for professional growth, including networking opportunities, mentorship programs, and career advancement workshops. Engaging with SWE not only allows me to advocate for gender diversity and inclusion in STEM fields, but also provides an environment where I could network with like-minded peers, learn insights from industry leaders, and contribute positively to the engineering community and society at large.

Built By Girls

Built By Girls is a vibrant community dedicated to empowering young women and non-binary individuals in technology and entrepreneurship. I gained mentorship, access to networking events, and skill-building workshops that supported me to navigate the tech industry and pursue my passions. Involvement with Built By Girls provides me with opportunities to connect with like-minded peers, as well as inspires me to challenge stereotypes and create a significant influence in the tech industry.
